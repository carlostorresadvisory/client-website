The process

From first contact to your decision.

What happens, with method and no surprises, after we talk.

  1. 1

    First conversation

    I take in the company and its context, listen to your objectives and priorities, and set out the best way forward for what you want to achieve.

  2. 2

    Proposal

  3. 3

  5. 5

Questions

What owners usually ask me.

And if a doubt remains, we clear it up in a conversation.

I'm not sure what I need.

Almost no one is, at first.

And you don't need to be. My work begins precisely there: making sense of the situation, separating the urgent from the important, and seeing what's worth doing and what isn't. Often, the answer is simpler than it seems.

I've received an offer for my company.

Yes, best read closely before you negotiate.

Almost no offer is fully understood on a first read: price is only part of it. I review the real scope, financial and legal, so you reply knowing exactly what's on the table and what can be improved.

I want to know what it's worth, with no commitment.

No figure commits you to anything.

Many owners value their company simply to know where they stand, plan a succession or negotiate with a partner as an equal. A valuation is information; what you do with it is your call.

I need this to be confidential.

It's the basis of everything I do.

Before I see a single number we sign a confidentiality agreement. I work alone and with complete discretion: your name and your figures never leave this conversation.

Why you and not my bank or my usual adviser?

Because all I sell you is judgement.

Your bank places products and your adviser runs the day-to-day; both do their job well, but neither looks at your company the way someone about to buy it would. That perspective, the other side's, on your side of the table, is what I bring.

How are fees determined?

Transparently, and always in advance.

Analysis and valuation are done on fixed fees, agreed before we start. In a transaction, part of the fee is tied to closing: my interest and yours point the same way. No surprises, at any stage.

I'd rather decide calmly.

That's exactly how it should be done.

No important corporate decision is made in a hurry. My role is to give you the information and the judgement to decide when you're ready, not before.
