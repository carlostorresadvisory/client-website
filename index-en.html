About me

Why I do this work.

Selling a company is often one of the most important decisions in an owner's life. It deserves the same rigour applied to large transactions, and that rigour should be within reach of an SME, not only a large corporation.

My background is that of a transactions analyst —a Master in Finance from IE Business School and CFA Level II passed— with work on real valuations alongside the Alantra team and in investment analysis at AFI.

Questions

What's worth knowing.

Why value a company?

To decide with judgement, not only to sell.

Knowing its value helps you plan growth, bring in a partner or prepare a succession. A sale is only one of the options.

Why consider selling?

It is rarely just about retiring.

Diversifying wealth, bringing in a partner, financing growth or ordering the succession are common reasons. Preparing is not selling: it is arriving with more options.

Who do you work with?

With owners of profitable SMEs.

Above all, consolidated family businesses whose owners are considering selling, bringing in a partner or understanding their company's value.

What documentation do I need?

Less than you imagine to begin.

The accounts, the ownership structure and the main contracts are enough to start. I tell you what we need at each stage.

Does valuing my company mean I have to sell it?

No, not at all.

The valuation is independent of any decision. Many value only to understand their situation; the decision is always yours.

Is the process confidential?

Completely, at every stage.

A confidentiality agreement is signed before sharing anything sensitive, and no buyer receives information without your go-ahead.

Do I need the documentation ready before talking?

No. The first conversation requires no documents.

We start by talking; when we begin a valuation, I guide you step by step.
