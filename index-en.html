About me

Why I do this work.

Selling a company is often one of the most important decisions in an owner's life. It deserves the same rigour applied to large transactions, and that rigour should be within reach of an SME, not only a large corporation.

I work alone, and only for you. No products to sell, no crossed mandates and no third-party commissions. Only judgement, independence and discretion.

The process

From first contact to your decision.

What happens, with method and no surprises, after we talk.

  1. 1

    First conversation

    I understand the company, your objectives and your priorities, and I explain how I can add value.

  2. 2

    Proposal

    I define the scope, the timeline, the fees and the confidentiality agreement. Everything is clear from the outset.

  3. 3

    Analysis

    I carry out the agreed work personally, with rigour and attention to detail. I ask only for the information needed and stay in contact throughout.

  4. 4

    Delivery & strategy meeting

    We go through the conclusions together: what your numbers say, where the risks are and what decisions you can make. The meeting matters more than the report.

  5. 5

Questions

What owners usually ask me.

And if a doubt remains, we clear it up in a conversation.

I'm not sure what I need.

That's the most common starting point.

Most conversations begin exactly there. The first meeting exists to order the situation and see what makes sense and what doesn't.

I've received an offer for my company.

It's worth reading closely before you reply.

An offer is rarely fully understood at first sight. An independent read of its economic and legal scope gives you the perspective to reply from the right position, not from haste.

I want to know what it's worth, with no commitment.

Knowing the value commits you to nothing.

It helps you plan, negotiate with a partner or simply have a basis for judgement. What you do with that figure is always your decision.

I need this to be confidential.

It is, by definition.

Before any sensitive information is shared, a confidentiality agreement is signed. No one knows we spoke unless you decide otherwise.

Why you and not my bank or my usual adviser?

Because I represent no one but you.

Your bank has products; your adviser, the day-to-day. My job is to look at your numbers with the eyes of a buyer or an investor, and tell you what I see. No other agenda.

How are fees determined?

Each project has a different scope.

Analysis and valuation work is carried out on fees agreed in advance. When you decide to execute a corporate transaction, the structure may include a success fee tied to closing. It's all defined clearly before we start.

I'd rather decide calmly.

That's exactly how it should be done.

No important corporate decision is made in a hurry. My role is to give you the information and the judgement to decide when you're ready, not before.
