About me

Why I do this work.

Selling a company is often one of the most important decisions in an owner's life. It deserves the same rigour applied to large transactions, and that rigour should be within reach of an SME, not only a large corporation.

My background is that of a transactions analyst —a Master in Finance from IE Business School and CFA Level II passed— with work on real valuations alongside the Alantra team and in investment analysis at AFI.

I work alone, and only for you. No products to sell, no crossed mandates and no third-party commissions. Only judgement, independence and discretion.

The process

From first contact to your decision.

Questions

What owners usually ask me.

And if a doubt remains, we clear it up in a conversation.

I'm not sure what I need. Can I still talk to you?

Of course. That's how most things start.

Most conversations begin exactly there. The first meeting exists for that: to understand your situation and see, honestly, whether it makes sense to work together. No commitment.

I've received an offer. Does it make sense to review it before replying?

Yes — and the sooner, the better.

Many deals begin exactly like this. An independent second look helps you see the real scope —financial and legal— before you commit to anything. Replying calmly almost always works in your favour.

Does asking for a valuation commit me to selling?

Not at all.

Many owners value simply to know where they stand: to plan, to bring in a partner, or just for peace of mind. What you do next is entirely your call.

Is all of this confidential?

Completely, from minute one.

Before I see a single number we sign an NDA. No one knows we spoke unless you want them to. Your discretion is non-negotiable.

Why you and not my bank or accountant?

Because I work only for you.

Your bank has products to place; your accountant, the day-to-day. I only have your interest — and the time to look at your numbers with the depth they deserve.

How are fees determined?

Each project has a different scope.

Analysis and valuation work is carried out on fees agreed in advance. When you decide to execute a corporate transaction, the structure may include a success fee tied to closing. It's all defined clearly before we start.

And if you can't help me?

I'll tell you, and that's fine.

If I can't add value, I'll say so in the first conversation. I'd rather have an honest relationship than a forced engagement. Often, that candour is where a long relationship begins.
